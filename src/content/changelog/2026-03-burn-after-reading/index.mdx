We've completely redesigned how secret links work when the recipient opens them. Instead of a plain text reveal, your recipient now sees a focused, single-use page that makes it crystal clear the information will vanish after viewing.

## What changed

Previously, opening a secret link showed the content right away on a standard page. It worked, but there was no visual cue that this was a one-time event. Recipients sometimes didn't realize the link wouldn't work again.

Now, the page shows a clear countdown and confirmation that the content has been revealed exactly once. After viewing, the link returns a "this secret has been destroyed" message — no ambiguity.

## How it works

1. **Create a secret** as usual — paste your text, set an optional passphrase
2. **Share the link** with your recipient
3. **They open it once** — the content appears with a clear "viewed" confirmation
4. **The link self-destructs** — any future visits show a "destroyed" notice
5. **You get notified** (if you're signed in) that the secret was viewed

<Callout type="tip">
  You can still set an expiration time as a backup. If the recipient doesn't open the link within your timeframe, it's destroyed automatically.
</Callout>

This feature is available on all plans, including free accounts.
